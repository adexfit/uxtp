UNSOLICITED ECOMMERCE LANDING PAGE REDESIGN

  1. Home
  2. Ecommerce landing page redesign

Overview

As I was surfing the internet to get royalty-free images for a project, I stumbled across a website 'pixels.com'. Pixels is the world's largest art marketplace and print-on-demand technology company. They have been helping artists sell wall art, home decor, apparel, and other products since 2006 and are home to hundreds of thousands of artists, photographers, graphic designers, illustrators, and iconic brands.

On examining the website, I Identified few problems which may limit the revenue of the company and also give the visitors a bad experience on the website's landing page.


Before redesign
Click to expand


The problems

The issues I identified are as follows:

  • The banner on the page looks clustered and there is no good visual hierarchy. This will leave visitors confused on visiting the landing page.
  • The colour scheme of the website was not fully maximized on the landing page.
  • Misleading onboarding page
  • Not all the links on the header can be accessed while scrolling. This means that if a user is at the bottom of the page, he will have to scroll up to access them


My solutions

On seeing the likely problems that users are likely to face on this website, I decided to do an unsolicited redesign of this ecommerce landing page. The following are the adjustments I made on the landing page:

  • I changed the color of the name of the Website and the shopping cart icon from black to the green hover color on the website to improve the overall look and to also make them stand out.

  • I removed the clustered images on the banner and replaced it with a single clean image. This will reduce the distractions of the users and help them focus on the more important items on the banner

  • I centrally positioned the call to action button and also included an opt-In form. This will help the user flow and build the mailing list of the company by making it possible for them to repeat customers.

  • I added more padding to the website to make it look cleaner and prevent content from clinging to the sides of the user's device.

  • In introduced a fixed header which will make it easy for the users to access all the links in the header irrespective of their position on the body of the website

Color palette






Conclusion

I strongly believe that the changes made in this redesign are capable of increasing sales and the user experience on pixels. They will help the company to easily build their email list and also give the users good experiences on the website.
